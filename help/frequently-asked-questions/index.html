Troubleshooting

4 Total

Why does the game feel slow on my computer?

If you are noticing issues while playing the World of Cars, it could be for one of many reasons. Please double check that your computer meets our system requirements to play this game. If you are still experiencing issues, you can Report a Bug.

click to close
What should I do if I can't connect to a server?

1. This might be a problem on the World of Car's end, not yours. The server might be offline so that we can update or work on the Web site. If you can get the World of Cars Online Community section, click on "Crews News Blog". You can find important news there about what's happening on the site.

2. Check to make sure you are using the latest version of Adobe Flash Player. Your Flash Player is the part of your computer that lets you play the World of Cars Online. You might need to install the newest version. The best way to do that is to go to the Flash Player Download Center.

3. Your computer might have a firewall built into it. Programs such as Windows XP, Vista, and Mac OS X have a firewall that might prevent you from logging on to World of Cars Online. Talk to a parent about how to change the settings on your firewall.

click to close
What do I do if I'm trying to race/drive but my Car won't move?

This could be a problem with the servers that run the game. The World of Cars Online needs regular maintenance and updates. We try to schedule these when the fewest Cars are online - usually late at night on the weekends. Cars that are online will always be notified that these short, but necessary, shutdowns are coming. Occasionally our servers get the hiccups, which can cause them to unexpectedly shutdown. We do our best to keep that from happening and try to fix any problems faster than you can cross the finish line. Thank you for your patience!
